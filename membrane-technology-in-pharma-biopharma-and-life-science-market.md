<p><p>Membrane technology in the pharma, biopharma, and life science markets encompasses various filtration processes. Microfiltration removes larger particles and microorganisms, while ultrafiltration separates proteins and smaller solutes. Nanofiltration targets divalent ions and small organic molecules, enhancing purification. Reverse osmosis provides high-level purification by separating solvent from solutes at the molecular level. Additional methods, like gas separation and electro-spinning, also play a role. These technologies are critical for ensuring product quality, safety, and efficiency in drug development and manufacturing.</p></p>
