<p><p>Membrane separation technologies include various processes for separating substances in liquid solutions based on size and charge. Reverse Osmosis (RO) utilizes a semipermeable membrane to remove dissolved salts and impurities, making it ideal for desalination. Ultrafiltration (UF) filters larger particles, such as colloids and proteins, while Microfiltration (MF) targets even larger contaminants, like bacteria. Nanofiltration (NF) bridges the gap between RO and UF, selectively removing divalent ions and small organic molecules. Other technologies encompass emerging methods and specialized applications in various industries.</p></p>
