<p><p>Fluid Viscous Dampers (FVDs) are devices that absorb energy by using viscous fluid to dissipate motion and reduce vibrations in structures such as buildings and bridges. The market for FVDs is divided into two primary types: Linear and Non-linear. Linear FVDs provide constant damping force regardless of the displacement or velocity, making them predictable and easier to model. Non-linear FVDs, on the other hand, offer variable damping characteristics, enabling adaptive responses to varying loads and enhancing performance in complex dynamic environments.</p></p>
