<p><p>The small animal anesthesia ventilators market features two primary types: machine-controlled and hand-controlled anesthesia ventilators. Machine-controlled anesthesia ventilators automate the delivery of anesthetic gases and assist in maintaining proper ventilation, allowing for precise settings and continuous monitoring, ideal for surgeries requiring consistent levels of anesthesia. In contrast, hand-controlled anesthesia ventilators require manual operation, giving veterinarians direct control over the gas flow and ventilation rate, making them suitable for situations where immediate adjustments are necessary or for mobile veterinary practices.</p></p>
