<p><p>Ice Storage Air Conditioning (ISAC) systems utilize two primary market types: Static Ice Making and Dynamic Ice Making. </p><p>Static Ice Making involves the production of ice in large, pre-formed blocks or containers, typically during off-peak hours, to be used later for cooling. </p><p>In contrast, Dynamic Ice Making generates ice continuously while simultaneously cooling the space, allowing for more flexible ice utilization and operational efficiency. Both methods aim to reduce energy costs and enhance cooling performance in various applications.</p></p>
